Analytics

I have been interested in statistics and analytics ever since I received 'MicroLeague Baseball' for my Atari 400 back in high school.

Each year I would purchase 'The Sporting News Baseball Guide,' create the World Series winner and Blue Jays team, and then simulate March Madness style tournaments (the 1927 Yankess won a lot of these).

These days I enjoy 'Out of the Park Baseball' as well as reading websites like FiveThirtyEight and FanGraphs and using software like Tableau at work.

When I come across something that I find interesting in the world of analytics I'll discuss it here.

Atari XL/XE

My very first computer was an Atari 400, where I learned how to write basic programs and began using a computer regularly.

In this section I'll discuss the tools I use and the programs I've written using modern computers that run on the Atari 8bit line. There is a great community out there who continues to write games and utilities for the older machines, of which I consider myself a proud member.

Cord Cutting

I walked away from cable back in 2014 and haven't looked back. Here I'll discuss how I've made that work, what I do and don't like about it, and anything interesting I stumble upon in the news related to cable alternatives.
