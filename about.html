Really About Me

I owe a lot to the Atari 400 that my dad purchased in 1982.

Unlike most kids around my age, I actually didn't get the games myself, it was my dad who bought the diskettes and swapped games with his buddies. So I did miss out on this part of the experience to some degree, but I still had a computer to mess around with, type magazine programs into, and experiment with.

My love of computers led me to the University of Waterloo and their B.Math program. Five years later I was working as a developer and my career working with computers officially began.

Between that point and today is a lot of water under the bridge not worth mentioning. All I'll say is that I (eventually) plan on doing some/all/or barely any of the following:

  • Writing some code that would automatically take baseball data from the web and create an MicroLeague GM disk
  • Creating an updated version of the game that includes some of the more popular advanced metrics (BABIP, FIP, etc.)
  • Porting the Atari800 emulator to the OpenPandora hand held (this has already been done but I'd like to implement my own UI)
  • Creating and documenting original games created using languages such as Action! or TurboBasic XL.
  • Creating my own XL database, using Python and MySQL, that will help me organize my 'collection' online

All of this is, of course, utter nonsense, because I'll likely start bits here and there and never finish any of it. But at least I'll remember what I planned on procrastinating for a change...
